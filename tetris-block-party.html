Case study · 2024-2026

Tetris Block Party

RoleCreative Product Manager
StudioPLAYSTUDIOS
BrandTetris® · Worldwide
PlatformiOS · Android

We turned the classic falling-block Tetris into a brand-new casual block-puzzle game. You don't just clear lines, you build your own party town, steal from rivals and attack their parties, all powered by a full suite of live-ops features that keep the world alive.

As Creative Product Manager, I owned the creative direction and the game requirements, and helped shape the new Tetris narrative and its cast of characters, writing the specs that kept design, art and engineering pulling in the same direction. I was responsible for bringing the game to soft launch, and for designing the features built to hit our KPIs.

It was all built with the worldwide Tetris® brand. Tetris and the Tetriminos are trademarks of Tetris Holding, licensed to The Tetris Company and sub-licensed to PLAYSTUDIOS. Every screen had to feel unmistakably Tetris while inventing a fresh block-party world of its own.

Meet the cast

The new narrative is carried by a crew of Tetrimino characters who bring the city to life: Ian the I-piece, Oskar the O-shape, Lizzy the Z-piece and Taylor the T-piece. Each piece has its own personality and its own role in the game, turning an abstract puzzle into a world with real heart.

Drop. Clear. Win.

The heart of the game is pure, tactile puzzle: place blocks on the grid, complete rows and columns, and race a win-multiplier target. Boosters, an energy meter and a punchy clear animation make every round feel rewarding. Easy to pick up, hard to put down.

Build your block town

Every win feeds the build. Coins from the board let players rebuild themed districts (like the Mariachi desert town) structure by structure; finishing a set completes a party, unlocks the next area and caps off with a celebration moment. It's the long arc that turns a quick puzzle into a world worth coming back to.

Steal & attack

The party gets competitive. Players go head-to-head, landing a Strike that disrupts a rival's board, raiding to steal their rewards and crashing their party, with REVENGE rematches raising the stakes. It's the social-competitive hook that keeps the rivalry, and the retention, alive.

Better with friends

It's a block party, after all. Players invite friends, join each other's parties, send and accept requests, and climb leaderboards together, turning a solo puzzle into a social meta-game.

Keep the party going

Daily streaks, streak-freezes, limited offers and rotating events form the live-ops layer: bright, generous, well-timed moments that give players a reason to return every single day.
